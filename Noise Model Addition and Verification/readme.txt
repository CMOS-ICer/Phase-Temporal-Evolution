These two "_Diff" files correspond to the method proposed in this paper and the two-frame subtraction method, respectively; they contain the velocity calculation results—including Monte Carlo simulation data—incorporating the noise described in Section 5. The outputs from these files support the results presented in Fig. 8, Fig. 9, and Table 3 providing data for metrics such as RMSE and MAE. In `Frame_Diff.m`, the equivalent noise level at a specific distance can be adjusted by modifying `sigma_ref_m` and `d_ref_m` (the default setting is 6 mm of equivalent noise at a distance of 0.9 m). Noise parameters in `Row_Diff.m` are configured by modifying `d_start_m` and `sigma_start_m`. Additionally, parameters such as exposure time and modulation frequency can be adjusted in both files. The data used by these files originates from Mitsuba outputs; please refer to the `readme` file within the `mitsuba` folder for details. The `readNPY.m` script facilitates the parsing of Mitsuba output files in `.npy` format.
